About the Organisers

College of Engineering Trivandrum (CET) was established in 1939 as the first Engineering College in the state of Kerala. The institute was started as a constituent college of Travancore University. With the establishment of the Directorate of Technical Education in the late fifties, the college administration came under the control of the Government of Kerala. The college spread out in an 80 acre campus is located 12 km from the city centre and 10 km from the Trivandrum International Airport.

The college is the most sought after engineering college by engineering degree aspirants in the state. There are eight full-fledged departments offering 7 undergraduate and 21 postgraduate programmes under the University of Kerala. The college has around 3500 students, 285 faculty members and 290 non-teaching staff. The institute with its strong research base and extensive infrastructural facilities, offers Ph.D. programmes in a wide range of areas in Engineering and is recognised as QIP (Quality improvement Programme) centre for research leading to Ph.D.

The college has collaboration with many leading institutions including Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre, Thiruvananthapuram and C-DAC Thiruvananthapuram. MOUs have been signed with twenty institutions for joint research work and faculty/research scolar sharing. The Centre for Engineering Research and Development (CERD), Government of Kerala extends the necessary support to the college

The ICCC 2015 is being organised by three departments of the college: Department of Electrical Engineering, Department of Electronics & Communication Engineering and Department of Computer Science & Engineering.
